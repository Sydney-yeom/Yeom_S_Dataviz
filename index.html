The introduction for these analyses

Q. What are these analyses?

These are the data analyses of Asian countries' Olympic medals from 1956 to 2014 by using 'OlymicsWinter' data, provided by Professor Joe Kormendi. It is composed of four different analyses; it is how the number of medals is composed by gender, how the portion of the medal count is different between 'before 2000' and 'after 2000', the portion of the medal count by sports with gender, and lastly the portion of the medal number by sports with countries. By the way, Asian countries' athletes started to gain medals from 1956.

1. The entire medal count composition by gender

This chart shows the composition with the number of males' medals and females' medals between 1956 and 2014. Overall, it represents that the males got slightly more awards than females in total. To be exact, the number of males' medals is 125, and the amount of females' medals is 117. By the way, four countries(CHN, JPN, KAZ, KOR) got medals in the males game competition, and six countries(CHN, JPN, KAZ, KOR) got awards in the females game competition between 1956 and 2014. The second chart(2. The medal composition by gender with countries) shows a more detailed portion of the medal count.

2. The medal composition by gender with countries (1956-2000, 2000-2014)

In detail, according to these charts for more detailed figures, these charts show how the data of the first chart(The chart 'The medal count comparison') are composed. Before 2000, the males got almost twice as many medals as the females did. By the way, the males got 62 medals, and the females received 37 medals. But, after 2000, the females received about 20 more awards at 80 medals than the females between 1956 and 2014.

From 1956 to 1998, Japan recorded an overwhelmingly high score, at 37 medals among the Asian countries in the male game part, and both Korea and China accounted for the most scores, at 14 medals in the female game part. On the other hand, the countries that got the lowest medals are Kazakhstan, at 4 medals in the male game competition; also, both Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan earn one medal in the female game.

Unlike 20th's medal data, after 2000, Japan's score decreased to 13 from 37, and Korea made up the highest scores, at 27 medals from 15 medals in the male game. Also, both China and Korea's scores increased more than doubling since the 20th, but China shows the highest score among the other countries, rose from 14 medals to 40 medals. Also, Kazakhstan shows the lowest score again in both males and females game. Their grades fell from 4 to 1 in the male games and stabilized from 1 to 1 in the female game.

3. The portion of the medal number by sports with Gender

Overall, it represents that both men and women got the highest scores overwhelmingly, at 87 medals and 101 medals in total in the skating game. Moreover, the men's medal number in skiing accounts for 38 medals, and the number of women's' medals in the same sports occupies 10 medals. Also, in particular, while the women got the prizes through the three sports, skating, skiing, and curling, the men received the medals through the two sports, skating and skiing.

4. The portion of the medal number by sports with Countries

It tells that most countries won the prizes from the sports 'skating' in the both charts. In particular, according to the left chart for the females, it can be seen that China and Korea got almost similar scores over 40; To be exact, China's medal score is 44, and Korea's medal score is 45. Through the chart, only China won the prizes for 'curling', and also, both Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan has fewer medals than the other Asian countries, at just one medal for 'skiing'.

Comparison China and Uzbekistan

While Uzbekistan is the one country that has the fewest medals among Asian countries between 1956 and 2014, China is the one that has the most medals at 54 medals. There are as many as 53 medals between them. Also, the sports that they got the prizes from are different; Uzbekistan got a medal for 'skiing', and China got 44 medals for 'skating', 5 medals for 'curling', and 5 medals for 'skiing'. Comparing their medal scores with only skiing, China got 4 more awards than Uzbekistan.
